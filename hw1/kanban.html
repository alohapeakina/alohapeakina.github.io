Kanban Overview

Kanban is a practice born out of the Toyota Production System, sometimes also called the "Just-in-Time" production system, which was developed and used by Toyota to streamline its manufacturing systems. Extending from concepts such as Lean Thinking, Kanban emphasizes three key practices:

Kanban Practices

1

Defining and visualizing a workflow

2

Actively managing items in a workflow

3

Improving a workflow

Kanban's focus on visualization helps practicioners to clearly see the work required, providing easy ways to identify bottlenecks or inefficiencies in a process. This often takes the form of a "Kanban Board" (see image below).

Each column within a Kanban Board represent a stage in a workflow and are populated through cards which indicate tasks that move through the process (To Do → In Progress → Done). To emphasize completion of work, each phase of a process generally has a limit to the number of items which can be in that phase, encouraging the full completion of tasks before new ones are started.
